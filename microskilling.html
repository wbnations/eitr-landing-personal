Microskilling

You don't need a certification. You need the next right move.

The Philosophy Behind the Navigator

Microskilling is the practice of acquiring a small, specific, immediately applicable skill — rather than completing a lengthy course before you can act. In the age of AI, where tools evolve faster than curricula, microskilling is how professionals stay current without falling behind on the work that matters today.

Every skill in the AI Skills Navigator is designed to be used within 24 hours. Not someday. Not after the next training cycle. Today. Each module asks one question: what is the smallest thing you could learn right now that would make tomorrow's work measurably easier? That question is the entire philosophy.

How It Works

1

Pick Your Role

Start with who you are at work, not what AI can theoretically do. The Navigator is built around roles — IT Manager, Business Leader, Student/Athlete, IT Professional, Sales Professional — because the right skill depends entirely on what you actually do each day.

2

Choose Your Level

Foundations, Proficiency, or Expertise — based on where you actually are, not where you think you should be. Most people start in Foundations regardless of seniority. The habits you build there are what make the higher tiers work.

3

Do One Thing

Each module is 2–40 minutes. Complete one. Apply it. Come back for the next. The system is designed so that doing one thing is always enough — and never the last thing you want to do.

The biggest barrier to AI adoption isn't complexity. It's the feeling that you have to understand everything before you can do anything.
