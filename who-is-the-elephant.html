The Elephant

Everyone's talking around it. We're talking about it.

It's AI. And you're not alone.

The elephant in the room in every workplace right now is artificial intelligence — the fear that it's replacing jobs, making skills obsolete, or is simply too complex to learn. It shows up in meetings where nobody asks the obvious question. In job postings that suddenly mention Copilot. In the colleague who's quietly getting twice as much done and not explaining how.

But the data tells a different story. The people falling behind aren't the ones who tried AI and failed. They're the ones who never started. Every week they wait, the gap between them and their AI-fluent peers grows wider — not because the technology is hard, but because the first step is.

This series exists to close that gap. Through five character-driven stories set in real workplaces and real scenarios, Elephant In The Room shows what it actually looks like to go from avoidance to adoption. No hype. No buzzwords. Just people figuring it out — in IT, in the boardroom, in the classroom, and on the sales floor.

Why We Tell Stories

We don't learn from statistics. We learn from people who look like us, face problems we recognize, and find solutions we can actually use. The brain doesn't retain abstract principles — it retains characters, conflicts, and moments of change.

Each episode of Elephant In The Room is built around a real AI skill challenge mapped to a real role. The learning is embedded in the story — not bolted on afterward. When Gerald uses AI to triage a ticket crisis, you don't just watch it happen. You understand why it works and how you'd do it yourself. That's the difference between a training and a story worth sharing.

Our Mission

To make AI skill-building feel less like training and more like a conversation you actually want to have.
